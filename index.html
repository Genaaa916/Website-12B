3D rendering in architecture

What is 3D rendering?

3D rendering is a process of turning information from a 3D model into a 2D image.
Often the image is produced in the hopes of making it look realistic, therefore simulating a real photographic image.

How is 3D rendering useful in architectural design?

3D rendering is a valuable tool often used in architecture. It plays a key role in both exterior and interior design, letting the designer see a finished design before having to physically execute a design.
This way, the designer can play with different options, such as sizes, color patterns and designs before implementing them.

How else is 3D rendering used?

3D rendering is also an effective marketing tool.
Imagine you're building a housing complex, and you want to put condos up for sale. Traditionally, you can only sell tangible things - things that the end user can see and/or touch.
Therefore, selling a condo that isn't yet built would be impossible. However, with 3D rendering, the sales process can begin before the complex is even built.
Photorealistic renderings of a flat are a great tool for pre-marketing of said flat, and give customers an accurate idea of what the end product will look like.
